Upon successful completion of this course, the student should be able to:

  1. Demonstrate estimation skills.

  2. Justify results of estimation.

  3. Use dimensional analysis to convert units of measurements.

  4. Use fractions, percentages and proportions to solve contextual problems.

  5. Compute geometric measurements or perimeter, area, volume, and angles.

  6. Use technology to analyze and interpret elements of personal finance.

  7. Compare and contrast measures of center and measures of dispersion.

  8. Interpret tables, charts, and graphs.

  9. Use tables, charts, and graphs to communicate results of data.

  10. Apply quantitative reasoning to solve problems.
